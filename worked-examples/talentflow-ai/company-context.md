## What we sell

TalentFlow AI is an AI-powered sourcing and screening platform that helps talent acquisition teams find, qualify, and engage candidates faster — without sacrificing the human judgment that determines whether a hire actually works.

**One sentence:** TalentFlow AI reduces time-to-qualified-slate by 60% by automating the research and initial screening work that currently consumes most of a recruiter's day — while keeping humans in control of every decision that matters.

---

## How it works

TalentFlow connects to a company's ATS (Greenhouse, Lever, Workday) and job descriptions, then continuously sources candidates from public profiles, talent databases, and passive candidate signals. For each open role, it builds a qualified slate — candidates ranked by fit score, with a structured profile showing relevant experience, likely motivations, and suggested outreach personalization.

For screening, TalentFlow conducts AI-assisted async interviews: structured question sets calibrated to the role, with responses analyzed for role-relevant signals. Recruiters review a synthesis, not a transcript — they see what matters, not everything that was said.

Critically: TalentFlow surfaces recommendations. Humans make decisions. No candidate is advanced, rejected, or contacted without recruiter review and approval.

## Key differentiators

**1. Human-in-the-loop architecture by design**
TalentFlow is not an autopilot. Every sourcing recommendation, every screening synthesis, every outreach draft requires recruiter review before action. This is not a limitation — it is the product philosophy. Enterprise buyers navigating AI governance requirements and candidate experience concerns need this assurance. It is built into the architecture, not bolted on as a disclaimer.

**2. Fit scoring grounded in role context, not resume keywords**
Most AI sourcing tools match keywords. TalentFlow matches against a structured role profile — the skills, behaviors, and experience patterns that predict success in this specific role at this specific company — built from the job description, hiring manager input, and historical hire data where available. The difference in signal quality is significant.

**3. Screening synthesis, not transcripts**
Async interview tools give recruiters more data to process. TalentFlow gives them less — specifically, the structured synthesis of what matters for the hiring decision. Recruiters spend time evaluating candidates, not reading transcripts.

## What TalentFlow is NOT

- Not an ATS — it integrates with your ATS, it does not replace it
- Not a background check or assessment platform
- Not an autonomous hiring system — no candidate is advanced without human review
- Not appropriate for high-volume hourly hiring with no recruiter review (our model requires human oversight at every stage)
- Not a job board or sourcing database (we source from existing databases and public profiles; we do not own a talent network)

## Voice and tone

**Tone:** Warm, credible, and practitioner-level. We talk to recruiters and TA leaders who care deeply about people — not just efficiency. The language of "speed" and "automation" without the language of "quality" and "human judgment" will lose them.

**Always:**
- Acknowledge that hiring is a human decision with real consequences for real people
- Pair every efficiency claim with a quality or experience claim
- Use specific metrics — not "faster hiring" but "60% reduction in time-to-qualified-slate"
- Lead with the recruiter's experience, not the technology

**Never:**
- Use "automate hiring" — we augment recruiters, we do not automate decisions
- Imply that AI screening replaces human judgment
- Use "AI will find your best candidates" — AI surfaces candidates, humans evaluate them
- Reference speed without referencing quality in the same breath

**Approved terminology:**
- We say "qualified slate," not "candidate list"
- We say "screening synthesis," not "AI interview"
- We say "recruiter review," not "human approval" (less clinical)
- We say "fit scoring," not "ranking" or "rating"
- We say "human-in-the-loop," not "human oversight" (the latter sounds like monitoring, not partnership)
